# Survey Methodology

This document describes the user survey that motivated the dataset,
including the instrument, the recruitment process, sample
characteristics, and known limitations, since survey instruments can
themselves introduce bias and deserve explicit scrutiny.

## Instrument

An anonymous structured questionnaire was administered through Google
Forms. The questionnaire covered self-reported interest in and knowledge
of Brazilian financial products, usage frequency, perceived fraud and
scam risks, comfort with banking acronyms, and open-ended descriptions
of recent financial experiences.

## Recruitment and sample

- Collection window: July to September 2025.
- Valid responses: 118.
- Geography: predominantly the Brazilian Northeast, with 48.3% in Recife
  (PE) and 14.4% in Mossoró (RN).
- Age: 83.9% in the 19 to 35 range.
- Education: 74.5% at or beyond higher-education level.
- Distribution channel: convenience-based online sharing through academic
  and institutional networks associated with UFPE and ICTi.

## Headline findings that shaped the dataset

- Financial literacy gap: mean self-reported interest score of 4.17
  against a knowledge score of 2.81 on a five-point scale (gap of 1.36
  points).
- Fraud and coercion vulnerability: 40.7% reported fear of fraud, 26.3%
  reported fear of scams, and 20.3% reported fear of physical coercion
  during financial transactions.
- Acronym comprehension: 28.1% reported not fully understanding common
  banking acronyms (CDB, LCI, LCA).
- PIX adoption among surveyed products was the highest (4.34 of 5), which
  is consistent with the over 76% national adoption figure reported in
  Banco Central do Brasil PIX statistics.

## Known limitations

- Convenience sampling introduces selection bias: respondents are
  over-represented in the Northeast and under-represented in the
  South and Central-West.
- The sample skews young and educated relative to the broader Brazilian
  banking population; low-literacy users are likely under-represented.
- Online administration excludes respondents without reliable internet
  access, which is a relevant share of the population the paper
  discusses.
- Self-reported frequency and comfort are subjective and can be
  affected by social desirability bias, even with anonymous responses.

## Mitigations adopted

Questions that made it into the PIX-Br dataset were validated against
official regulatory documentation (Banco Central do Brasil), not against
the survey responses themselves. The survey was used as motivation and
as a source of realistic language patterns and concerns, not as a source
of ground truth. Ground-truth answers for all 500 dataset instances are
derived from published PIX regulation.

## Release

The anonymised survey instrument is not redistributed here because the
raw questionnaire responses contain free-text fields that could contain
quasi-identifiers. A request process for academic reuse is available by
contacting the authors directly.
